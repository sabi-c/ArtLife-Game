# 🎭 Event Types

## Overview
Events fire between turns and are the primary way the world interacts with the player. They range from social gatherings to personal life shocks. Each event can trigger dialogue choices, market shifts, or both.

---

## Event Categories

### 🥂 Cocktail Party
> *The real deals happen over drinks.*

- **Frequency:** Every 3–6 turns
- **Trigger:** Random, or unlocked by social perk
- **What Happens:**
  - Meet NPCs (other collectors, artists, dealers, critics)
  - Receive tips, gossip, or market intel
  - Build or damage relationships
- **Player Choices:**
  - Who to talk to (limited time slots)
  - Whether to act on a tip immediately or hold
- **Outcome Examples:**
  - "A dealer whispers an artist is about to be shown at MoMA" → Buy signal
  - "A rival collector is offloading their whole collection" → Possible deal

---

### 🖼️ Gallery Opening
> *First look, first mover advantage.*

- **Frequency:** Every 4–8 turns
- **Trigger:** Tied to artist heat cycles or calendar events
- **What Happens:**
  - New works available at pre-market prices
  - Limited quantity — not everyone gets to buy
  - Access gated by relationship with that gallery
- **Player Choices:**
  - Buy now (pre-heat, lower price, higher risk)
  - Pass (wait to see if artist breaks out, pay more later)
- **Outcome Examples:**
  - Buy early on a nobody who becomes hot = massive return
  - Buy early on a dud = dead weight in portfolio

---

### 🏛️ Art Fair
> *Art Basel, Frieze, TEFAF — the annual pilgrimages.*

- **Frequency:** ~Every 52 turns (annual)
- **Trigger:** Calendar-based
- **What Happens:**
  - Multiple galleries in one event — high density of buying opportunities
  - Major price signals set for the year
  - NPC networking opportunities are abundant
  - Press attention affects artist heat scores
- **Player Choices:**
  - Which galleries/booths to visit (limited slots)
  - Whether to buy at fair prices or wait for post-fair corrections
- **Outcome Examples:**
  - Artist featured prominently → immediate heat spike
  - Quiet fair for a once-hot artist → heat decline signal

---

### 🔨 Auction
> *Public, transparent, and brutal.*

- **Frequency:** Every 10–20 turns (major houses), random for smaller sales
- **Trigger:** Calendar + seller activity
- **What Happens:**
  - Works go to the highest bidder
  - Prices are public — sets market comparables for all similar works
  - Winning too aggressively inflates the market (good if you hold, bad if you want to buy more)
- **Player Choices:**
  - Set a max bid
  - Walk away if price exceeds value
  - Strategically bid up a rival's desired lot
- **Outcome Examples:**
  - Your artist's work sells for 3x estimate → your holdings spike in value
  - Work bought cheap at distressed auction → immediate upside

---

### 📦 Freeport Opportunity
> *Store it, forget about it, profit later.*

- **Frequency:** Every 15–30 turns, or on-demand
- **Trigger:** Player-initiated or event-prompted
- **What Happens:**
  - Option to move works into a freeport (Geneva, Singapore, Delaware)
  - Removes works from active market visibility
  - Defers tax obligations while in storage
- **Player Choices:**
  - Which works to store vs. keep accessible
  - How long to hold in storage before selling
- **Outcome Examples:**
  - Store 5 works for 3 in-game years → sell tax-efficiently at peak
  - Keep in freeport too long → miss selling window during hot market

---

### 💀 Personal Life Event
> *Life doesn't stop because the market is moving.*

- **Frequency:** Rare (every 30–80 turns), class-dependent
- **Trigger:** Character-specific probability
- **Examples:**

| Event | Class | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Father dies of heart attack | Rich Kid | Inherit collection + cash |
| Annual bonus hits | Hedge Fund | Large cash injection |
| Divorce settlement | Any | Forced sale of works |
| Artist you championed turns on you | Gallery Insider | Relationship damage |
| Tax investigation | Speculator | Freeze on certain assets |
| Rival acquires your target artist | Any | Blocked from certain works |
