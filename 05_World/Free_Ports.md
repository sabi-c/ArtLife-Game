# 📦 Freeports

## What is a Freeport?
A freeport is a tax-free storage facility where art can be held indefinitely without triggering import duties or capital gains tax. In the real world they exist in Geneva, Singapore, Luxembourg, and Delaware. In the game, they are a core strategic mechanic.

---

## Why Use a Freeport?

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Tax deferral** | No capital gains until you remove and sell the work |
| **Discretion** | Works held here are off-market and not visible to rivals |
| **Long-term holds** | Ideal for works you want to sit on for years |
| **Freeport-to-freeport sales** | Works can be sold between freeports without leaving — no tax triggered |

---

## Freeport Costs
- **Storage fee** per work, per turn (scales with work value)
- **Transfer fee** when moving work in or out
- **Entry cost** — must have relationship with a freeport operator (unlocked via perk or event)

---

## Strategic Use Cases

### The Long Hold
- Buy an emerging artist's work early
- Move to freeport immediately
- Sit for 3–5 in-game years while heat builds
- Sell from freeport when market peaks
- **Result:** Maximum appreciation, minimum tax

### The Invisible Collection
- Store significant holdings in freeport
- Rivals and market can't see what you hold
- Maintain information asymmetry
- **Result:** Strategic advantage in negotiations

### Freeport-to-Freeport Transaction
- Sell a work to another collector — both in freeport
- Transaction happens on paper, no physical movement
- No tax triggered for either party
- **Result:** Liquid market even for very high-value works

| Location | Access Requirement | Special Feature |
|---|---|---|

| Singapore | Hong Kong connections | Best for Asian market works |
| Delaware, USA | New York base | Easiest entry, lower fees |

---

## Risk Factors
- Regulatory event (rare): government scrutiny of freeport holdings — forced disclosure
- Fire/theft event (very rare): loss of uninsured work
- Over-storage: holding too long while fees accumulate can erode returns
