# ⏱️ Time System

## Core Concept
Time in the game is compressed. Each **turn = 1 week** in-game, but real-world art market cycles (years of holding, artist trajectories, economic booms/busts) play out across those turns at speed.

---

## How It Works

### Advancing Time
- Player clicks **"Advance Week"** button to move forward one turn
- During the transition, **no player actions are possible**
- At the end of each week, the game delivers:
  - Market price updates
  - Triggered events
  - NPC relationship changes
  - Any scheduled bonuses or income

### Time Scale Reference

| In-Game Time | Real Turns |
|---|---|
| 1 month | ~4 turns |
| 6 months | ~26 turns |
| 1 year | ~52 turns |
| 5 years | ~260 turns |
| 10 years | ~520 turns |

---

## Why This Matters for Strategy
- An artist you discover early might take **3–5 in-game years** (156–260 turns) to break out
- Holding a piece through a market correction requires **patience across turns**
- Annual events (Art Basel, auctions, bonuses) recur every ~52 turns
- Players can plan long-term without real-world waiting

---

## Fast-Forward Option (Future Feature?)
- Could allow players to skip ahead in bulk (e.g. "Skip 4 weeks") at the cost of missing events
- Risk/reward: speed vs. missing opportunities
