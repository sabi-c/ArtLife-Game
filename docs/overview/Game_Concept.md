# 🎨 Game Concept

## Elevator Pitch
A **Pokémon-style art world RPG** where you walk through galleries, negotiate deals in email, research the market on a retro computer terminal, and build a collection worth millions — all within a compressed, fast-moving version of the real contemporary art world.

> See [Gameplay Loop](Gameplay_Loop.md) for the full experience design.

---

## Core Loop

```
HOME (Player's Apartment)
  └─→ Computer Terminal (Win95 OS)
        ├── Art Terminal (Bloomberg/Artnet) ── research, trade, track
        ├── Email ── negotiate deals, receive offers
        └── Instant Messenger ── NPC gossip, tips
  └─→ Walk the City (Pokémon overworld)
        ├── Galleries ── browse, buy, talk to gallerists
        ├── Art Fairs ── Frieze/Basel booth walk
        ├── Auction Houses ── bid on lots
        ├── NPC Offices ── private haggle battles
        ├── Parties ── networking, eavesdropping
        └── Studios ── commission work, build relationships
  └─→ Return Home → Advance Week → Weekly Report → Repeat
```

---

## Design Pillars

1. **Spatial Presence** — You physically walk through the art world. Galleries are real places you enter and explore.
2. **Digital + Physical** — The computer terminal is your research hub; the streets are where deals happen.
3. **Social Capital Matters** — Who you know affects what you can access and at what price.
4. **Risk vs. Patience** — Holding is powerful but costs opportunity. Timing is everything.
5. **Character Identity** — Your starting persona shapes your entire experience and available strategies.
6. **Emergent Narrative** — Random life events (inheritance, scandal, death, bonus) make each run feel unique.
7. **Living Market** — NPCs trade autonomously, creating a dynamic economy that reacts to player and AI actions.

---

## Genre DNA
- **Pokémon** — walk the world, encounter NPCs, turn-based battles (haggle)
- **Stardew Valley** — daily routine, relationship building, seasonal events
- **Recettear** — buy/sell loop, haggle mechanics, market timing
- **Offworld Trading Company** — market tension, speculation
- **Crusader Kings** — character-driven, emergent story
- **Uplink** — retro computer interface, hacking aesthetics
