# 📜 Scenarios & Story Events

## Overview
Scenarios are larger, multi-turn narrative arcs that unfold across several weeks. Unlike single dialogue cards, scenarios have a beginning, middle, and end — with multiple decision points along the way.

---

## Scenario List (Seed Ideas)

---

### The Breakout Artist
> *An unknown artist you may or may not hold is on the verge of breaking into the mainstream.*

- **Trigger:** Random, or via Gallery Insider tip
- **Arc:**
  1. Rumour surfaces at a cocktail party (buy signal)
  2. Gallery announces a solo show (heat +15)
  3. Major institution acquires a work (heat +25)
  4. Art fair feature — heat peaks (sell window)
  5. Post-peak correction — latecomers get burned
- **Stakes:** Timing is everything — early buyers win big, late buyers overpay

---

### The Market Crash
> *Something external has rattled the market. Prices are falling across the board.*

- **Trigger:** Macro event, every 80–120 turns
- **Arc:**
  1. Warning signs — a few auctions underperform
  2. Crash hits — all works lose 20–40% value
  3. Panic selling begins — distressed works available cheap
  4. Recovery phase — smart buyers who held or bought cheap win
- **Stakes:** Holding through a crash vs. cutting losses — classic investor dilemma

---

### The Rival Collector
> *A new player has entered your market. They're well-funded, aggressive, and targeting the same artists.*

- **Trigger:** Mid-game, after player builds significant portfolio
- **Arc:**
  1. Rival appears at same events, outbids you on a desirable work
  2. You can ignore, compete, or try to build a relationship
  3. If you compete — bidding wars inflate prices (good for your existing holdings, bad for new buys)
  4. If you ally — potential to co-invest or share intel
  5. Resolution: one of you dominates a particular artist's market
- **Stakes:** Competition vs. cooperation, ego vs. strategy

---

### The Artist Relationship
> *You've been buying an artist's work consistently. They've noticed.*

- **Trigger:** Buy 4+ works by the same artist
- **Arc:**
  1. Artist reaches out via their gallery — invites you to their studio
  2. Studio visit — you can buy a work directly (below market)
  3. Artist asks for your support in a public way (lend work to show, speak to press)
  4. Decision: be a patron or stay transactional
  5. If patron: artist loyalty, future access, heat boost from association
  6. If transactional: fine, but artist may start selling to others first
- **Stakes:** Relationship capital vs. financial returns

---

### The Inheritance Arc (Rich Kid Class)
> *Your father's health is declining. The estate is complex.*

- **Trigger:** Rich Kid class, ~Turn 60–100
- **Arc:**
  1. Family lawyer contacts you — father's affairs need attention
  2. You can engage (learn estate details) or ignore
  3. Father dies — estate event fires (see Dialogue Trees)
  4. Estate settlement takes several turns — works are locked
  5. Tax bill arrives — must sell something or take a loan
- **Stakes:** Major windfall but with strings attached

---

### The Scandal
> *An artist you're heavily invested in is at the centre of a controversy.*

- **Trigger:** Random, higher chance for heavily-held artists
- **Arc:**
  1. Rumour surfaces
  2. Press picks it up — market nervousness begins
  3. Crisis point — sell, hold, or defend?
  4. Resolution: vindicated (heat recovers + bonus for those who held) or confirmed (heat crashes)
- **Stakes:** Information vs. risk tolerance
