## 🔑 Key Decisions Made

### Use Real Artists & Real Data
- Game should use **real artist names** — keeps it topical and grounded
- Feed from **ArtNews Top 100** lists (collectors, artists) for name pools
- Use **real auction results** to inform in-game market values and price benchmarks
- This means the game feels current, not fictional — a key differentiator

### Art Drama Feed
- Real-world art drama (lawsuits, scandals, forgery cases, market crashes) = source material for events
- Debrief the **last ~10 years of art world drama** and encode as event templates
- Not a "daily news feed" in-game — but a historical **event library** that the game draws from
- Events feel like they *could* happen because they *did* happen (with names/details changed or kept)

### Start Text-Based, Layer Visuals Later
- MVP = pure **text-based decision strategy game** first
- Get the decision maps, trees, and core loop working in text
- Pixel art / visual polish comes after the logic proves fun
- This is the cheapest, fastest way to validate the concept

## 🎮 New Mechanics Ideas

### Gambling Side-Mechanic
- Player can **gamble** as an offshoot activity
- Could be: poker at a collector dinner, casino during Art Basel, sports betting in Monaco
- Risk/reward mechanic: fast cash or fast losses
- Ties into character personality and reputation

### Art Identification Mini-Game
- A piece of art **slowly reveals** on screen
- Player guesses the artist or title — faster correct answer = more points/money
- Could be: trivia at gallery events, authentication challenges, "is it real or fake?"
- Connects to knowledge as a game mechanic — the more you play, the more you learn

### Vacation / Travel Scenarios
- "You're on vacation in St. Barts" — social encounters at luxury locations
- Chance to network, discover works, make deals outside normal market channels
- Could tie into annual calendar (summer = Hamptons/Mediterranean, winter = Miami/Basel)

## 📈 Progression Design

### Level Tiers
- **Early game:** Getting into the art world — learning, small purchases, building first relationships
- **Mid game:** Established player — making strategic moves, attending major fairs, building a collection
- **Late game:** Power player — influencing markets, rival dynamics, legacy decisions
- Progression gates: certain events/locations/mechanics **unlock** as you level up
- Prevents overwhelming new players with complexity

### Interconnected Scenarios
- Scenarios shouldn't be isolated — they should **chain and overlap**
- Meeting someone at a cocktail party → leads to a studio visit → leads to an investment opportunity
- Creates a web of consequence, not just a series of one-off events
